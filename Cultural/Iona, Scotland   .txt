Iona is an enchanting island located off the west coast of Scotland, famous for its spiritual and historical significance. The island is home to the impressive Iona Abbey, a 12th-century structure that is considered the birthplace of Christianity in Scotland, as well as the Celtic crosses, gravestones, and ruins that make up the ancient cemetery surrounding the abbey. The island boasts breathtaking natural scenery, with white sandy beaches, crystal clear waters, and rolling hills dotted with grazing sheep. It is also an ideal location for birdwatching, hiking, and exploring the diverse marine life.
