Patmos is a charming island in Greece, situated in the Aegean Sea. The island has a rugged terrain with rocky hills and stunning beaches. It is known for its stunning white-washed houses and quaint alleyways, that add to its Mediterranean charm. The island is home to many historic buildings and landmarks, including the Monastery of Saint John, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the Cave of the Apocalypse, which is said to have inspired the Book of Revelation. Visitors can also enjoy delicious Greek cuisine at local tavernas and indulge in the island's thriving nightlife scene. Patmos is a must-visit destination for those seeking a tranquil and culturally rich Greek vacation experience.
