Sabarimala is a small town located high in the hills of the Western Ghats in the Indian state of Kerala. It is renowned as a major pilgrimage site for Hindus, with thousands of devotees visiting the Sabarimala temple every year. The journey up to the temple is a breathtaking experience, with a steep climb through lush forests and picturesque mountain scenery. The temple is dedicated to Lord Ayyappa, and the atmosphere around the temple is serene and spiritual, making it a must-visit attraction for tourists seeking a taste of Hindu culture and religion. The town also offers plenty of small shops selling souvenirs and local cuisine.
