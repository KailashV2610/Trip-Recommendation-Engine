El Yunque National Forest is a lush rainforest located in northeastern Puerto Rico. As a tourist, you'll be greeted with an array of breathtaking waterfalls, hiking trails, and picturesque vistas. The forest is home to a diverse range of plant and animal life, which includes over 240 species of trees and rare bird species such as the Puerto Rican tody. The park also features historical sites, such as the landmarks of Yokahu Tower and La Mina Falls, which date back thousands of years. You'll surely enjoy the unique sights and sounds of El Yunque National Forest, a natural haven in the tropics.
