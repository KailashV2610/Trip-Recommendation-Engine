The Great Ocean Road is a scenic coastal drive in Australia that offers breathtaking views of the Southern Ocean, rugged cliffs, and lush forests. The 243 km route winds past charming seaside towns, wildlife sanctuaries, and iconic landmarks such as the Twelve Apostles - towering limestone stacks that rise from the ocean. The road is dotted with numerous lookouts that provide panoramic vistas of the coastline and make for great photo opportunities. Visitors can also take part in a range of activities, including surfing, hiking, and wildlife watching. The Great Ocean Road is a must-visit destination for any tourist looking for a quintessential Australian experience.
